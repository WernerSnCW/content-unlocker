# Unlock Decumulation Engine — Technical Specification
Version: 1.2 | March 2026

## Purpose

The Decumulation Engine answers the question every investor
approaching retirement needs to ask: given my assets, how long
will my money last if I spend £X per year — and what is the
optimal sequence to draw from those assets to minimise tax and
maximise what I pass to my heirs?

## Build Status

Specification complete. Prototype build commencing.

## Asset Classes Covered

The engine models full UK tax treatment per asset class including
wrapper override rules:

- Cash
- ISA (including flexible ISA)
- Pension (DC/SIPP/workplace)
- Investment property
- VCT (Venture Capital Trust)
- EIS (Enterprise Investment Scheme shares)
- AIM shares

IFAs cannot advise on VCT/EIS drawdown sequencing — this is a
structural advice gap that the Decumulation Engine fills.

## Four Drawdown Strategies

The user selects a strategy; the platform models all four for
comparison. Each produces a different asset draw priority order.

1. Tax-optimised — minimise income tax and CGT drag across the
   full drawdown horizon
2. IHT-optimised — maximise the estate passed to heirs by
   preserving BPR-qualifying assets for as long as possible
3. Income-first — prioritise immediate income stability over
   tax optimisation
4. Growth-first — preserve higher-growth assets longer,
   drawing from lower-growth assets first

## Key Modelled Scenarios (Toggleable)

- Proposed April 2026 BPR cap: £2.5M at 100% relief, 50%
  relief above that threshold
- Proposed April 2027 pension IHT inclusion — models both
  current rules and proposed rules simultaneously for comparison
- Trust and gifting mechanics: NEFI, CLT 7-year rolling window
  with NRB stacking, PET taper relief schedule
- Year-indexed tax parameters for multi-decade plans
- 35-year shadow projection always runs alongside the user's
  selected plan horizon

## Why No Incumbent Has Built This

The Decumulation Engine requires lot-level data from the asset
register — which platforms with product conflicts cannot provide
without exposing competitor holdings. A platform that earns from
recommending products cannot model "draw from your ISA before
your pension to preserve BPR exemption on your VCT" without
undermining its own revenue model. Structural independence is
what makes the Decumulation Engine possible.

## Compliance Notes

All modelled scenarios carrying legislative uncertainty must
include the following caveats where presented to investors:

- BPR cap: "Subject to final enactment"
- Pension IHT: "Subject to final legislation"
- All tax figures must match compliance_constants.json values
  and include appropriate adviser confirmation caveats

## Target Personas

Most relevant to:
- Preserver (late accumulation and decumulation phases)
- Legacy Builder (late accumulation through decumulation)

Most valuable at pipeline stages:
- Demo Complete
- Decision
