## THE ONE-LINE BRIEF

**Institutional clarity. Human directness. Zero padding.**

Unlock speaks like a very smart friend who happens to understand tax and investment at an expert level. Not a financial institution. Not a salesperson. Not an academic. The friend who gives you the real picture and trusts you to handle it.

---

## 1. VOICE

### What Unlock sounds like

**Confident without being arrogant.**
We make strong claims because they're true and we can prove them. We don't hedge unnecessarily, and we don't overclaim. "The UK regulatory moat" is a real structural advantage — say it plainly.

**Direct without being blunt.**
Short sentences. Active voice. The point first, the explanation second. Never bury the lead.

**Intelligent without being complex.**
Our audience is sophisticated. They don't need hand-holding, but they do need clarity. Institutional-grade intelligence, expressed in plain English. If a sentence needs re-reading, rewrite it.

**Honest without being apologetic.**
We're a founding-stage platform. We say so. We don't dress it up or minimise it. Sophistication means acknowledging risk — not hiding it.

**Urgent without being pushy.**
Genuine urgency only — April 6 deadlines, BPR rule changes, valuation step-ups. Never manufactured scarcity, countdown clocks, or artificial pressure. Sophisticated investors see through it immediately and it destroys trust.

## 2. PERSONA-MATCHED TONE

*The voice stays consistent — the emphasis shifts by audience.*

| Persona | Pace | Lead with | Key words |
|---|---|---|---|
| **The Preserver** | Slower, measured | Safety, validation, control | "peace of mind", "risk controls", "confirmed", "visible" |
| **The Growth Seeker** | Faster, energetic | Opportunity, transparency, access | "upside", "deal flow", "syndicates", "informed" |
| **The Legacy Builder** | Measured, strategic | Long-term, family, timing | "inheritance", "succession", "tax efficiency", "timing matters" |

**On calls:** Match the investor's language back to them. If they say "down-risk", use "down-risk". If they say "succession planning", use "succession planning". Never impose vocabulary on a sophisticated investor.

## 3. WRITING RULES

### Structure
- **Point first.** Lead with the claim or conclusion. Evidence follows.
- **10-heading rule.** Any document longer than two pages should be readable in full by reading the headings alone. Headings carry the argument — they don't just label a section.
- **Two-minute companion.** Every long-form document has a 400–600 word standalone version. It is not a summary — it is a complete piece that works without the parent document.

### Sentences
- Short. One idea per sentence as a rule.
- Active voice. "Unlock models your IHT position" not "Your IHT position is modelled by Unlock."
- No throat-clearing. Never open with "In today's complex financial landscape..." — get to the point.

### Paragraphs
- Three sentences maximum in investor documents.
- One idea per paragraph.
- White space is not wasted space. It aids reading for a busy, senior audience.

### Words to use
- "Founded investors" not "seed investors" or "angels"
- "Instant Investment" — always, every time, for the investment instrument
- "Growth Capital round" — never "Series A"
- "Founding-stage" — for platform status
- "Defined criteria" — for the 24 Access criteria (not "scored" or "evaluated")
- "Administration" — for the fourth Access pillar (not "Execution")
- "investors in the know" — approved framing for EIS-aware audience
- "the only mechanism by which investors can reduce their tax liabilities to zero" — approved phrasing, use verbatim or not at all

### Words never to use
- "Game-changing", "revolutionary", "disruptive", "world-class" — unsubstantiated superlatives, zero in any Unlock document
- "Independent" — for Unlock Access specifically (Access raises for its own pipeline companies — this is stated openly, not hidden, but "independent" is not accurate)
- "Simple" or "easy" — patronising to a sophisticated audience
- "Exciting" — amateur
- "Unique" — overused, meaningless; state the specific differentiator instead
- "Leverage" as a verb
- Exclamation marks — zero in investor documents

*Use these formulations. Do not paraphrase or approximate.*

| Topic | Use this | Never use |
|---|---|---|
| Investment instrument | "Instant Investment" | ASA / Advanced Subscription Agreement / SAFE |
| Round name | "Growth Capital round" | "Series A" |
| Investor label | "Founding investor" | Seed investor / early investor / angel investor |
| Access fourth pillar | "Administration" | "Execution" |
| EIS certificate | "EIS3" | "EIS13" |
| Discount tiers | "Negotiated individually based on strategic value" | Publish specific % figures |
| Scarcity framing | "~25 slots by design — quality control, not artificial scarcity" | "Only 3 slots remaining!" / countdown clocks |
| Unlock Access independence | "Full transparency. Unlock Access raises for its own pipeline companies. This is stated openly." | "Independent" |
| EIS loss relief | State the bracket: "~27.5p per £ (additional rate)" | "20p on the pound" / "30p in the pound" as if universal |
| Zero liability | "the only mechanism by which investors can reduce their tax liabilities to zero" | Any variation using "potentially" |
| IFA positioning | "Structural partners — they fill an adjacent role" | "Competitors" / "the problem" |
| Financial projections | "Management estimates" / "forward-looking projections" | "Targets we will hit" / any certainty framing |
| Tom's dual role | Frame as a structural advantage with clear rationale | Hedge, apologise for, or omit |

| Public launch date | "Public launch targeted Q1 2027" | "Launching soon" / "Coming soon" / "Almost ready" |

## 6. WHAT WE NEVER DO

**Manufacture urgency.** Countdown clocks, "only X slots remaining", false deadlines — none of these. Genuine urgency exists (April 6 tax year, BPR rule change, valuation step-up). Use that.

**Minimise risk.** Sophisticated investors read risk sections specifically to find what the founder is glossing over. State the real risks, follow with mitigations. Omission is worse than disclosure.

**Overstate the platform.** We're founding-stage. Say so. Don't claim capabilities the platform doesn't yet have. Don't describe planned features as live.

**Advise.** Unlock is not FCA regulated and does not provide financial advice. Every investor-facing document carries a disclaimer. Content informs — it never advises.

**Patronise.** This audience manages £1M–£20M+ portfolios. They've had advisers, seen pitch decks, read prospectuses. Write for that level of sophistication.

## 7. FORMAT BY DOCUMENT TYPE

| Document type | Headings | Paragraphs | Lists | Tone |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Investor documents (100–190) | Yes — carry the argument | Max 3 sentences | Use sparingly — evidence or features only | Confident, direct, no filler |
| Email templates (230) | Subject line only | 2–3 short | One CTA maximum | Personal, brief, one ask |
| Agent scripts (500, 510) | Section markers | Conversational | Talking points, not essays | Spoken English — contractions fine |
| Internal documents (200–220) | Full structure | Standard | Extensive use fine | Functional, precise |
| Educational content (150, 160, 190) | 10-heading rule | Short | Use for evidence/comparison | Informed friend — not salesperson |
