## 1. PLAY TO WIN WORKSHOP

### A — The Framework

Play to Win (Roger Martin) is a cascade of five strategic choices that must be made in sequence. Each choice constrains the next. The output is a coherent, internally consistent strategy that explains not just what you're doing, but why you can win doing it.

**The cascade:** Winning Aspiration → Where to Play → How to Win → Capabilities Required → Management Systems

**The test of a good P2W:** Can you draw a straight line from your winning aspiration through every downstream choice? If any choice seems disconnected from the one above it, the strategy has a break.

**What P2W produces for Unlock:**
- The winning aspiration → goes into mission statement, Content Bank Section 1, all investor docs
- Where to play → drives segment definition, persona prioritisation, geographic scope
- How to win → drives product differentiation claims, competitive moat language
- Capabilities → drives roadmap priorities, hiring, Werner's build sequence
- Management systems → drives KPIs, review cadence, decision rules

### C — Population Rules: Play to Win

**If stuck on Choice 1 (Winning Aspiration):**
Finish this sentence: "We will know we've won when [specific change in the world] is true for [specific customer segment]." Then compress it.

**If stuck on Choice 3 (How to Win):**
Ask: "What do we have that our best competitor would need 18+ months and significant capital to replicate?" That is your moat. If the answer is "nothing yet," that is the most important thing to build.

**Discipline:** Play to Win choices must be revisited as a complete cascade when the business materially changes. Changing Choice 2 (where to play) without reviewing Choice 3 (how to win) produces drift. This review should happen: at each funding round, when entering a new geography, when adding a product category.

## 2. STORYBRAND SB7

### A — The Framework

Storybrand (Donald Miller) organises your communication around a 7-part narrative structure drawn from classic storytelling. The core insight: your customer is the hero, not your company. Your company is the guide. Most founders get this backwards and lose the audience.

**The 7 parts:** Character → Problem → Guide → Plan → Call to Action → Failure → Success

**What SB7 produces for Unlock:**
- The one-liner (already complete)
- The narrative structure for Pack 1 and all investor-facing docs
- The email subject line logic
- The demo script structure
- The agent call framework

**Key principle for Unlock:** The investor is the hero trying to protect and grow their wealth. Unlock is the guide with the tools and plan. The failure state (what happens if they don't act) is paying more tax than necessary and making decisions with incomplete information. The success state is the full financial picture, with clarity.

### C — Population Rules: Storybrand

**Common mistakes:**

1. **Making Unlock the hero.** "Unlock is a platform that..." — wrong. "Private investors with £1M+ portfolios face..." — right. Lead with the customer's problem, not your product.

2. **Skipping the internal problem.** The external problem (fragmented data) is easy to state. The internal problem (the feeling of uncertainty and nagging doubt) is what makes investors lean forward. Don't skip it.

3. **Plan complexity.** If the plan takes more than three steps to explain, the investor hasn't understood it. Simplify communication, not the product.

4. **Weak failure framing.** Failure must be real — genuine financial cost or missed opportunity. Never manufactured urgency. Sophisticated investors can smell manipulation.

**When to revisit:** When the one-liner stops landing in demos. When email open rates drop. When prospects consistently misunderstand what Unlock does. These are SB7 signals, not product signals.

## 3. VALUE PROPOSITION CANVAS

### A — The Framework

The Value Proposition Canvas (Osterwalder) maps customer jobs, pains, and gains against your product's pain relievers and gain creators. The goal is fit: the customer profile on one side, the value map on the other. Fit occurs when your pain relievers address the pains that matter most, and your gain creators produce outcomes the customer actually cares about.

**What VP Canvas produces for Unlock:**
- The core value proposition statement (one per persona)
- The pain/gain language for Pack 1 Section 2
- The by-persona VP for the 520 persona guide
- The objection responses in the call script

**This must be done once per persona.** A generic VP Canvas is worthless. The Preserver, Growth Seeker, and Legacy Builder have different jobs, different pains, different gains — and Unlock's value for each is different.
