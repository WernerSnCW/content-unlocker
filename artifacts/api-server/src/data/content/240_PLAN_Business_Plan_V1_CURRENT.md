## SECTION 1: EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

**Unlock** is a portfolio intelligence and investment access platform for UK private investors with £1M–£20M+ in total wealth.

**The Problem:** Private investors manage fragmented portfolios across multiple providers with no single view, no institutional-grade tax modelling, and no way to stress-test decisions before making them. Every existing platform earns from products, not investor outcomes. The conflict of interest is structural.

**The Solution:** Unlock assembles the complete financial identity — UK tax intelligence, forward-looking, investor-controlled — with no product conflicts, no commission, no AUM fee. Five core modules: Asset Register, Tax Intelligence Engine, Portfolio Simulator, Decumulation Planner, and Unlock Access (curated EIS deal-flow).

**The Market:** UK HNW private investors (£1M–£20M+ total wealth). The segment is under-served, well-defined, and large enough to build a significant business. No competitor is squarely positioned here.

**The Team:** Tom King (CEO/Founder, 15 years financial services), Werner Snyman (Head of Product/Development). Founding backers include the former Chairman of Barclays Stockbroking and the former Director General of TISA.

**The Ask:** £1.2M at £6.5M pre-money valuation. ~25 founding investors via Instant Investment (SeedLegals). Shares issued April 6, 2026. Public launch targeted Q1 2027.

## SECTION 3: THE SOLUTION

### Platform Architecture

Unlock is a next-generation portfolio intelligence and investment access platform. Five core modules:

**1. Asset Register** — Consolidates all asset classes, all providers, lot-level cost basis in one connected view. Local-first architecture (Electron/SQLite) automates data collection via browser automation on investor's own machine — the correct architecture for platforms without public APIs. Portfolio summary data pushed to cloud for web/mobile. Status: Operational for founding investor onboarding (white-glove).

**2. Tax Intelligence Engine** — Models EIS, SEIS, CGT, IHT, pension drawdown, AIM BPR continuously with live HMRC rule-tracking. Forward-looking, not backward-looking. The UK regulatory moat — 18–24 months minimum to replicate. Status: Decided capability. In active development. Full build funded by this raise.

**3. Portfolio Simulator** — Stress-tests portfolios under 30+ downside scenarios. Models what-if before committing capital. Macro belief integration (growth, inflation, rates, currency, credit). Status: Decided capability. In active development. Full build funded by this raise.

**4. Decumulation Planner** — Plans drawdown sequencing across all UK asset classes. Four strategies: tax-optimised, IHT-optimised, income-first, growth-first. Trust/gifting logic (NEFI, CLT, PET). Toggleable BPR cap and pension IHT scenarios. 35-year shadow projection. Status: Specification complete (v1.5). Prototype build commencing.

**5. Unlock Access** — Curated EIS deal-flow and portfolio service. Four pillars: Guidance, Tools, Access, Administration. Direct portfolio building — not a fund. 24 defined criteria disclosure framework. 8–10% fees vs 9–14% market norm. Full transparency: Unlock Access raises for its own pipeline companies — stated openly. EIS compliance via SeedLegals Instant Investment.

### Pricing Strategy

Unlock uses a **Quality Leadership** pricing approach — not penetration, not skim. Price signals institutional quality while remaining accessible relative to AUM-based alternatives:

- **Positioning:** "Institutional-grade tools. Without the institutional price or the institutional conflicts."
- **Price anchoring:** Traditional alternatives (private banks 1–2% AUM, wealth platforms 0.4–1% AUM on a £2M portfolio = £8K–£20K/year) vs Unlock (£1,188–£2,988/year for Standard/Premium)
- **Value-based framing:** Subscription replaces AUM fee entirely. Alignment with investor success, not assets under management.
- **Discount strategy:** Founding investors receive lifetime premium access. Post-launch, discounts negotiated individually based on strategic value — never published as specific % figures.
- **No price wars:** The competitive moat is structural (independence + regulatory depth + data flywheel), not pricing. Price competition would signal commodity positioning — the opposite of Unlock's thesis.

## SECTION 8: RISK FACTORS

*Stated honestly. Sophisticated investors read risk sections specifically to find what the founder is glossing over. Mitigations follow each risk.*

### 1. Execution Risk (Early Stage)
The platform is founding-stage. Public launch targeted Q1 2027. Full capability not yet live. **Mitigation:** Working product in use for founding investors. Complete technical specification for Decumulation Planner. Credible milestone roadmap. 40+ market interviews validating demand.

### 2. Regulatory Risk (EIS/SEIS/FCA)
HMRC or FCA changes to EIS/SEIS rules, AML/KYC requirements, or data protection regulations could affect the tax intelligence engine or investor eligibility. **Mitigation:** Platform designed for continuous HMRC rule-tracking. Regulatory change is built into the operating model. Compliance constants tracked and enforced via automated QC.

### 3. Market Adoption Risk
HNW investors are cautious. Trust takes time. If adoption is 20% slower than projected, break-even shifts to Year 3 rather than Year 2. **Mitigation:** Freemium model provides lower-friction entry. Business remains viable at slower adoption. Data flywheel creates increasing switching cost over time.

### 4. Technology Risk
Local-first architecture (Electron/SQLite) depends on browser automation for platforms without APIs. Upstream platform changes could break integrations. **Mitigation:** Architecture is correct for the problem — no alternative without storing credentials remotely. Integration monitoring and rapid remediation.

### 5. Key Person Risk
Tom King conducts all demos at founding stage. Credibility cannot yet be delegated. **Mitigation:** By design for founding round (25 slots, quality over volume). Scaling constraint to be resolved before Growth Capital round outreach at volume.

### 6. Competition Risk
Large platforms (HL, Interactive Investor) could attempt portfolio intelligence features. **Mitigation:** Product conflict makes genuine independence impossible for incumbents. UK tax complexity takes 18–24 months to build correctly. Data flywheel advantages early movers.

### 7. Market Downturn Risk
Significant downturn could reduce appetite for early-stage investing and alternatives. **Mitigation:** Counter-intuitively, portfolio stress-testing and downside modelling become more valuable in downturns. Risk management tools attract users when markets fall.

### 8. Data Accuracy and Cybersecurity
Sensitive financial data for HNW investors requires high standards of accuracy, uptime, and security. **Mitigation:** Standard technology risk managed through architecture decisions and security investment. Local-first architecture means sensitive data stays on investor's machine by default.

### 9. Series A Dependency
Full platform build requires follow-on funding. **Mitigation:** Break-even projected at Year 2 (~£3M ARR). Strong unit economics (LTV:CAC 10–15:1). Realistic pathway to profitability without Series A.
