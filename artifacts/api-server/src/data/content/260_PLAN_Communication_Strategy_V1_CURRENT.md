## 3. VOICE & TONE

### The One-Line Brief
**Institutional clarity. Human directness. Zero padding.**

### Voice Principles

| Principle | In Practice |
|---|---|
| **Confident without arrogant** | Strong claims because they're true. No unnecessary hedging. No overclaiming. |
| **Direct without blunt** | Short sentences. Active voice. Point first, explanation second. Never bury the lead. |
| **Intelligent without complex** | Institutional-grade intelligence in plain English. If a sentence needs re-reading, rewrite it. |
| **Honest without apologetic** | Founding-stage platform. Say so. Don't dress up or minimise. |
| **Urgent without pushy** | Genuine urgency only — April 6, BPR changes, valuation step-up. Never manufactured scarcity. |

### Tone by Persona

| Persona | Pace | Lead With | Key Words |
|---|---|---|---|
| **The Preserver** | Slower, measured | Safety, validation, control | "peace of mind", "risk controls", "confirmed", "visible" |
| **The Growth Seeker** | Faster, energetic | Opportunity, transparency, access | "upside", "deal flow", "syndicates", "informed" |
| **The Legacy Builder** | Measured, strategic | Long-term, family, timing | "inheritance", "succession", "tax efficiency", "timing matters" |

### Language Rules

**On calls:** Match the investor's language back to them. If they say "down-risk", use "down-risk". Never impose vocabulary.

**In writing:** Point first. 10-heading rule (headings carry the argument). Three sentences max per paragraph. One idea per sentence. No throat-clearing ("In today's complex financial landscape..." — never).

## 4. MESSAGING FRAMEWORK

### Primary Messages (in priority order)
1. Private investors deserve the same clarity that institutions take for granted.
2. Your wealth is scattered across platforms, advisers, and spreadsheets. Unlock brings it together.
3. Every other platform earns from what it sells you. Unlock earns only from you.
4. Institutional-grade tools. Without the institutional price or the institutional conflicts.
5. Know your complete financial picture before you act — not after.

### Narrative Structure (Storybrand SB7)

**Hero:** UK HNW private investor (£1M–£20M+ portfolio). Wants a complete, accurate picture of their financial position.

**Villain:** Conflicted platforms. Every existing tool earns from products, not investor outcomes. The conflict is structural.

**Problem:**
- External: Fragmented portfolios, no single view, no institutional-grade tools
- Internal: Uncertainty. Nagging sense of paying more tax than necessary, missing opportunities, not getting full picture
- Philosophical: Institutions have teams and dashboards. Private investors have spreadsheets. No reason this gap should exist in 2026.

**Guide:** Unlock. Empathy ("We built Unlock because we watched sophisticated investors making decisions with incomplete information") + Authority (15 years FS, Barclays/TISA backing, SEIS assurance, 40+ interviews, Decumulation Engine v1.5).

**Plan:** Three steps — (1) Get the complete picture (2) Model decisions before making them (3) Access the right opportunities on the right terms.

**Failure:** Investors without EIS/SEIS exposure paying income tax they don't need to. April 6 BPR cap creates structuring deadline. Founding terms never offered again.

**Success:**
- Preserver: Every position visible and stress-tested. Spreadsheet gone. Uncertainty gone. Institutional clarity.
- Growth Seeker: Deal-by-deal access. Real-time intelligence. Every allocation modelled before committed.
- Legacy Builder: Rolling EIS programme tracked. Estate tax position clear. Decisions documented. April deadlines never missed.

**Universal success line:** "Live well. Leave wealth. Stay safe."

## 5. CHANNEL STRATEGY

### Channel 1: Direct Outreach (Founding Round — Primary)

| Element | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Database** | 30,000+ UK HNW investor profiles (proprietary) |
| **Method** | Cold call (Aircall) → discovery call → demo booking → Pack 1 |
| **Cadence** | Systematic daily outreach. Track in Pipedrive. |
| **Messaging** | EIS/SEIS hook → pain identification → demo offer |
| **Content** | 100 (one-pager), 150 (EIS explainer) as leave-behinds |

### Channel 2: Email Sequences (230 Templates)

| Stage | Purpose | Content | Tone |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Stage 1 — Outreach** | Introduce Unlock | EIS/SEIS hook. One ask: book a call. | Personal, brief |
| **Stage 2 — Post-call** | Reinforce demo booking | Pain reflection. Persona-specific content. | Informed, relevant |
| **Stage 3 — Pre-demo** | Prepare investor | What to expect. No selling. | Professional, calm |
| **Stage 4 — Post-demo** | Send Pack 1 | Decision support. Urgency (genuine only). | Confident, direct |
| **Stage 5 — Decision** | Close | How to invest. SeedLegals process. Final deadline. | Clear, action-oriented |

**Email rules:** Subject line only (no headings). 2–3 short paragraphs. One CTA maximum. Personal, brief, one ask.

### Channel 3: Content Marketing (Post-Launch)

| Content Type | Purpose | Distribution |
|---|---|---|
| EIS/SEIS education | Top-of-funnel. Establish authority. | SEO, LinkedIn, IFA referral |
| Tax intelligence insights | Mid-funnel. Demonstrate platform value. | Email newsletter, SEO |
| Case studies | Bottom-of-funnel. Social proof. | Pack 1 companion, post-demo |
| Market commentary | Brand awareness. Thought leadership. | LinkedIn, email |

### Channel 4: IFA Partnership Programme (Post-Growth Capital Round)

## 10. WHAT WE NEVER DO

1. **Manufacture urgency.** No countdown clocks, no "only X slots remaining", no false deadlines. Genuine urgency exists — use it.
2. **Minimise risk.** State real risks, follow with mitigations. Omission is worse than disclosure.
3. **Overstate the platform.** Founding-stage. Say so. No capabilities described as live that aren't.
4. **Advise.** Unlock is not FCA regulated and does not provide financial advice. Every investor-facing document carries a disclaimer.
5. **Patronise.** This audience manages £1M–£20M+ portfolios. They've had advisers, seen pitch decks. Write for that level.
6. **Use prohibited terms.** "Game-changing", "revolutionary", "disruptive", "world-class", "simple", "easy", "exciting", "unique", "leverage" (as verb). Zero in any Unlock document.
7. **Use exclamation marks** in investor documents. Zero.

## 11. MEASUREMENT & OPTIMISATION

### Communication KPIs

| Metric | What It Measures | Action If Failing |
|---|---|---|
| Email open rate | Subject line effectiveness | Revisit SB7 problem framing — are we leading with the right pain? |
| Email reply rate | Message resonance | Check persona matching — are we sending the right message to the right audience? |
| Demo booking rate | Call effectiveness | Review pain identification and authority establishment in call framework |
| Pack 1 → decision rate | Document persuasion | Audit belief ladder — which belief is failing? Go back to VP Canvas. |
| Objection frequency by type | Message gaps | New objections → Content Bank Section 8 first, then propagate |

### When to Revisit This Strategy
- When email open rates drop below threshold
- When prospects consistently misunderstand what Unlock does (SB7 signal)
- When the one-liner stops landing in demos
- At each funding round
- When entering a new geography or adding a product category
